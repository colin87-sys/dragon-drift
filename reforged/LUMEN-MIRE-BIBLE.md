## 1. THEOLOGY SENTENCE

> **"Nothing here shines from the sky — the drowned forest makes its own light, every glow is a
> living thing, and the safe way through is written in the lanterns of the swamp."**

| Biome | Subject | Light source | Verb |
|---|---|---|---|
| Frozen | luminous ice | cool light lives IN the mass; warm only FROM the sun | lives-in / stillness |
| Caldera | black crust over living fire | fire from BELOW, through wounds | rises |
| Lost Lagoon | drowned masonry reclaimed | the low sun PASSES THROUGH apertures | passes-through / reclaims |
| Aurora | a dark still mirror | the SKY-CURTAIN overhead (solar wind) | reflects / holds-still |
| **LUMEN MIRE** | a living drowned forest | **the ORGANISMS — light is metabolic, from within life** | makes-its-own / lights-the-way |

Corollaries every element answers to (Fable, Ruling 1):
- **Mire keys the ORGANISM.** If a glow can't answer "what living thing is making you?", it is cut.
  No sun, no fire, no sky, no fabricated lamp — only gills, motes, threads, rotten-wood interiors.
- **Life glows; matter drinks.** Living tissue emits from within; dead/wet matter (bark, root, mud,
  water at rest) is near-black and light-absorbing. That split IS the value ladder (§3).
- **The light is the wayfinding.** The safe lane is a line of living lanterns; glow-colour = meaning.
- **The sky is never shown** — Mire has a roof, not a sky (§2, the Canopy Law; the anti-Aurora firewall).

## 2. EMOTION + THE MONEY SHOTS (composition is half the grade)

**Target emotion: LIVING-WONDER HUSH — "I've flown into a secret, breathing world that glows."**
(Frozen = lonely reverence; Caldera = standing inside something alive & indifferent; Lagoon =
arrival somewhere that was waiting; **Mire = wonder at a world that is quietly, everywhere ALIVE.**)

**THE CANOPY LAW (Fable Ruling 5 — the primary anti-Aurora lever, a hard structural law):**
> **Every frame's top edge is occluded by living matter — drape fringe, canopy mass, arch, or
> brim — covering ≥25% of frame height. Open sky is never composed. Mire has no sky; it has a roof.**

Binary-auditable per screenshot; survives Mire's coolest colour moments (Aurora is empty & still;
Mire's roof is dense & alive). **Engine reality:** there is NO per-instance altitude (`writeMatrix`
seats every prop at y=0, `environment.js:1021`); the roof is delivered by GEOMETRY that reaches
up/over the lane — Root-Gate arches, reaching frond/drape geometry on tall side instances, and the
Fog-Veiled Canopy Wall baked into the upper y-band on the horizon (§4). **Backup lever:** warm-gold
dominance (§3) + the amber-anchor rule (any cool moment still has amber motes in frame).

**Compositional grammar — THE LIVING CORRIDOR.** The eye reads a corridor whose WALLS and CEILING
glow (under-brim fungi, drape, arches) while its FLOOR (the black-mirror water lane) stays clean and
dark, doubling everything above it. Light falls DOWN from the brims onto the water and the mirror
throws it back UP — a light signature no other biome owns. Three-layer depth stack on every shot
(GoT/BotW, file 11 §3): near warm motes + drape fringe → mid silhouette screens (boles, arches,
knees) → soft amber-hazed horizon hero.

- **Money shot 1 — "THE FIRST LANTERN" (arrival).** ~200m of thinning fog + empty black mirror at
  the seam (arrival-park, §4). The first **Root Gate** resolves from the murk — a black cage of
  croissant-curve arches spanning the lane, feet in the mirror. **Through its largest arch-void, dead
  on the vanishing point, the Glow-Cap Colossus appears** as a soft amber dome miles off, gills
  underlighting its own fog halo, doubled in the water. A first ribbon of amber motes streams THROUGH
  the arch toward it (the drift-current as GoT Guiding-Wind leading line). Negative space: the arch
  interior holds ONLY the hero; below, one unbroken black sheet.
- **Money shot 2 — "UNDER THE BRIMS" (cruise signature / the postcard).** The dragon banks through a
  colonnade of **Shelf-Fungus Boles**; every shelf-tier is a warm underlit crescent, and their
  staggered under-brim glows form a **converging breadcrumb line of amber underlines to the horizon —
  the safe lane and the leading line are the same object**, pointing at the Colossus. Near: hanging
  moss + steady blue-white glowworm threads sweep past under the drape ceiling. The mirror doubles the
  whole colonnade. Negative space: the water corridor floor stays clean — glow lives on walls/ceiling.
- **Money shot 3 — "THE HELD BREATH" (negative-space hush + boss stage).** The mire opens to a wide
  still clearing: black mirror, one half-sunk foxfire log glowing mint from within, a thin ground-mist
  sheet, a sparse field of near-motionless amber motes each with its twin below. The drape thins to
  the Canopy-Law minimum (~25%). The NatGeo "found, not designed" frame.
  **RESERVED HORIZON EASEMENT (boss-stage law):** a permanent **~30° arc of horizon kept empty** — a
  gap in the Canopy Wall where fog stands as a pale amber-grey void, no landmarks/glow. This is
  THRUMSWARM's stage (its dark silhouette needs empty fog); the biome's drifting motes are its
  proto-form — pre-encounter they clump into the gap and organise into the boss's outline. No dressing
  pass may plant anything in the easement.

**Numeric composition targets at cruise (judge on captures pinned `?biome=4&debug`):** Canopy Law ≥25%
top-edge occlusion EVERY frame; ≥45% of visible water = unbroken black mirror; ≤3 step-1 emissive
clusters per frame (glow budget, §3); one hero-class mass per flank within 300m, alternating flanks;
the reserved 30° horizon easement prop-free; brightest non-emissive value stays in the darks (steps
5–6 genuinely black — contrast over brightness); every under-brim glow carries its reflection twin.

## 3. MATERIAL IDENTITY

**Value ladder — "LIFE GLOWS; MATTER DRINKS" (Fable Ruling 3; a per-part material split, not a
normal test).** This maps exactly onto the engine's hard 2-material cap (`mergeParts` ≤2 groups,
`environment.js:198`): every archetype is built from at most —
- **`mirePrimary` — DEAD/WET MATTER** (mat 0): near-black warm-neutral bark/root/mud. color
  `~0x0d1410`, low emissive `~0x0a1508 @ 0.10` (a floor so hemi-only faces don't crush, never a light
  source), `flatShading`, run through `addPropDetail` for free weathering richness. OPTIONAL subtle
  **`bakeMireLadder(geo, {stops})`** (a ~20-line sibling of `bakeIceLadder`, height-keyed, EXPLICIT
  swamp stops — dry-bark crown `0x1a2018` / wet-sheen waterline `0x0e1a16` / submerged-dark `0x060d0c`)
  to give the dark mass a damp carved read and dodge the Caldera "low-poly clay" r1 failure. ⚠ NEVER
  call `bakeIceLadder`/`bakeMireLadder` without explicit `stops:` (the Frozen blue-ice silent default).
- **`mireLiving` — LIVING GLOW** (mat 1): the emissive gill/mote material. color `0xffc23a`, emissive
  `0xffc23a → 0xf79a2e @ ~0.9`, low roughness for a soft not-hard glow; wrapped `withLadderEmissive`
  only if it carries a ladder. This is the ONLY emitter; rationed to glow-carriers (§4).

**The ladder, brightest → darkest (grade every shot):** (1) emissive cores — gills/motes/lane nodes
`0xffc23a`; (2) their reflections in still water (~70% core, broken where disturbed); (3) lit
mist/halos/god-shafts around clusters `0xf79a2e → 0x3a3324`; (4) mid vegetation catching spill
`0x243024`; (5) dead wet matter `0x05070a`; (6) resting black water & night `0x040914`–`0x0a0b12`.
**Glow budget: ≤3 hero-bright emissive clusters per frame; everything else is steps 2–4.**

**Accent + glow ADDRESS — UNDER THE BRIM (Fable Ruling 4).** One address: the downward-facing
undersides of every cap, shelf, and pad — gills lit from within, **recessed 15–25% of cap radius
inside the rim** so the cap's own overhang shadows them from directly above. From straight overhead a
glow-cap is a DARK disc with a faint warm rim-leak; from the flying ¾ vantage it's a warm underlit
shelf — glow earned by angle. Falloff brightest at the stalk-side gill root, fading to a soft warm
line at the rim (never a hard LED ring — the named kill-on-sight failure). Every under-brim glow gets
its reflection twin. Honors BIOME-DESIGN's reserved "Mire high under brims" address. Glow on the
glow-carriers only (§4); dark families carry none.

## 5. HAZARDS

### 5a. Three in-lane obstacle skins — `SKIN_BUILDERS[4]` (colliders byte-identical; fiction only)

Zero Frozen (calved bar / serac spur / berg shard) and zero Caldera (colonnade / spatter chimney /
breadcrust bomb) vocabulary. All: unit-space authoring, own numeric coverage exports + `hazardskin.mjs`
entries, silhouette contains the collider everywhere, magenta `0xff3dae` telegraph, under-brim glow
address, `mirePrimary`/`mireLiving` materials.

1. **BEAM → "DEADFALL SPAN."** A collapsed root-gate arch lying ACROSS the lane: a great curved
   knuckled bole bridging bank to bole, sagging mid-span, torn moss beards below. Its underside carries
   a run of shelf-fungus crescents glowing amber DOWNWARD — the glowing underline IS the kill-height
   read from distance. Telegraph: magenta lichen-vein pulse along the strike edge. Round/drooping,
   nothing prismatic. No spin (a fallen span doesn't roll).
2. **PILLAR → "DROWNED BOLE."** A buttress-foot trunk through the lane: melting-candle concave flares
   webbing into the water, asymmetric, bark-black. Glow: a ring of glow-moss at the waterline skirt
   (down-lit onto its own reflection) so it reads peripherally. Telegraph: magenta band at strike height
   on the lane-facing flank. Anti-basalt police: flares concave & knuckled, never faceted.
3. **SHARD (tumbling) → "SEEDPOD KNOT."** A dislodged giant swamp seedpod — a fist of curled husk-fins
   around a dense core — tumbling slowly, shedding a spiral of amber motes; faint amber gill-slits along
   the husk-seam undersides flicker as it rotates (organic strobing no ice-chunk/lava-bomb makes),
   bounds ≤1.25:1, wider than tall. **Dynamic variant: split the MATERIAL** — the core pulses magenta
   through the husk gaps ("same pod — it's about to burst"). ⚠ `hazardMesh` shard branch HARDCODES
   `mats.frostIce`/`mats.moverIce` (obstacles.js:427) — add a `bi===4 → mireHusk / mireHuskHot` route or
   it ships in Frozen ice.

### 5b. Native biome hazard — THE SPORE-VEIL (`hazard:{ type:'spore' }`)

The identity-triple hazard (BIOME-DESIGN §4), invented from the swamp. Presentation: clusters of
swollen **Smoker Pods** — puffball sacs bulging from bole flanks / deadfalls at lane height, skin taut,
faintly translucent, placed BESIDE the safe lane (slalom verb: the punished path clips them). Loop
(game-time, phase-offset per site, the proven `overlayBiomeHazards` pattern — own XOR'd `mulberry32`
stream `^0x?????`, own `out.hazards` array, consumption below the `inBoss`/grace returns in `main.js`,
cursor reset in `resume()`, gold-determinism byte-identical): IDLE → **WARN** (the pod's under-brim seam
pulses magenta `0xff3dae` on a rising rhythm, readable ≥90m) → **BURST** (~0.8s: a warm-grey spore cloud
`0x3a3324` body, amber-backlit edges, blooms across the lane — a soft brown whiteout, NOT a black
screen) → settle. Dodge-only, zero knockback, suppressed in-boss + grace, off tight ring lines.

**The navigation payoff (the verb made beautiful):** inside the veil, silhouette detail is gone but
**step-1 emissives survive as diffuse golden blooms** — the amber breadcrumb lane-nodes remain readable
through the murk (contrast over brightness kills shape-read, not glow-read), the mote drift-current
still leans down-lane (the Lost-Woods physics cue, readable from above), and breaking into clarity is a
designed relief beat. Flying the veil = trusting the lanterns. **FX law:** the cloud is a local opaque
particle core + rim-lit motes (overdraw law — NO enclosing additive shell, no global postfx vignette
that would die on tier 2 or break determinism); keep it a placed, local, deterministic object.

**Glow-code contract, enforced game-wide:** amber = safe/alive/path · blue-white = ambient life (never
navigational) · electric-blue = your own touch on the water · **magenta = danger, and magenta appears
NOWHERE else.**

## 6. ATMOSPHERE / AMBIENT

- **Sky:** a dark warm-neutral ROOF, mostly occluded (Canopy Law); stars ~0.2 glimpsed through gaps.
  No sky miracle — the biome's miracle is under-brim glow + the mirror + motes. (Departs hard from the
  shipped teal-night sky; the substrate PR must read as a NEW biome blind-tested vs Aurora BEFORE props.)
- **Water:** the black mirror, waveAmp ~0.18–0.25; reflection doubles the emissives (load-bearing).
  FOAM_CFG collars tuned to a faint warm waterline glow, not white surf; `canopywall` `foam:false`.
- **Fog:** dark humid warm-green near → amber-haze far; `heightK ~0.05` ground-mist pooling on water.
- **Particles — AMBER MOTES (the identity air):** `ambient{ color 0xffc23a, fall ~0.05 (near-hovering,
  a touch of rise), sway ~2.5 with a down-lane bias (the drift-current leading line), size ~0.5,
  opacity ~0.7 }`. Fireflies PULSE/DRIFT; they are the airborne amber identity and THRUMSWARM's
  proto-form. (Big change from the shipped mint motes.)
- **Fauna — the boss's children (later):** retool the glow-jelly flock toward slow drifting
  lantern-motes / lightning-bug clusters `{ color 0xffd24a, scale, flap slow }`; deepen with the
  foreshadow clumping in a later PR.
- **Audio (later, flagged):** a wet-ambience bed (water-lap, insect chorus, low frog drone) ~-18 LUFS
  under the radio; degrade to null when muted/headless.

**DEFERRED (per BIOME-DESIGN §10 — do not build in the look arc):**

  amber-anchor rule applies (amber motes must be in any frame the blue fires) so it reads BLUE not teal.
- **THRUMSWARM mote-clumping foreshadow** (motes organising into the boss outline in the easement):
  the BIOME-DESIGN §6 foreshadow channel — spec'd here, built with/after the boss coupling, not now.
