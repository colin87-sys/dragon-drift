# Phoenix build sheet: "compositional inversion" is a cheap, powerful distinctiveness lever

**What we did.** Authored the premium build sheet for Phoenix — the first dragon designed START-to-
finish under [`PREMIUM-DRAGON-METHOD.md`](../../reforged/PREMIUM-DRAGON-METHOD.md) (the playbook Solar
produced). Brief: "the opposite of Solar, majestic in her own fiery way — but NOT M wings." Flow: anchor
to the owner's 4 reference images → high-effort Fable synthesis (fed the images directly via the Read
tool) → owner confirms the load-bearing creative call → bank as
[`PHOENIX-DAWNFIRE-BUILDSHEET.md`](../../reforged/PHOENIX-DAWNFIRE-BUILDSHEET.md). No code yet — this is
the contract for the Phoenix session.

**The reusable design idea: to make dragon B unmistakably NOT dragon A, INVERT WHERE THE SILHOUETTE
MASS LIVES, not just the palette.** Solar's rear-chase frame is TOP-heavy (twin carpal spires + eclipse
ring crowned above the head; the tail was an afterthought). Phoenix's answer — "the Pyre-Train" — puts
the hero mass in the LOWER-REAR: a wide fanned train of discrete flame-quills below thin rising scythe
wings. Two silhouettes whose mass occupies opposite halves of the frame **cannot be confused at a
glance**, no matter how you light them. That is a stronger distinctiveness guarantee than a hue swap
(which the §0.5 gate warns is insufficient). The framing writes itself into identity too: Solar wears
his spectacle as a CROWN (above); Phoenix wears hers as a TRAIN (behind) — king vs empress.

**"Not an M" became a concrete geometry constraint, not a vibe.** The wings are single continuous raked
crescents with TERMINAL peaks (max height at the far outboard tip, monotonic from root) — the
mathematically OPPOSITE curve family to Solar's two-segment arch (interior peaks + a valley). "Different
silhouette" is enforceable when you state it as a different profile FUNCTION.

**The anti-tacky-fire solve worth reusing: "a coal, not a torch."** A fire dragon's trap is
everything-orange-everything-bright. The doctrine solve: keep the BODY dark (ash-garnet, L ≤ 0.22, the
diffuse ramp goes charcoal→garnet — REDDER, never lighter), and put fire ONLY on edges/tips/gems in
THREE spatially-separated warm hues (crimson pinion-fire / ember keel / amber-gold coal-eyes) so she
never smears into one blob. One tiny near-white element only. This is the same opaque-emissive doctrine
Solar used, re-proven for warm hues — and it's why the apex BODY does NOT go ivory (the shipped
"white-gold divine" form-3 was the toy-bright trap; retired).

**Process notes that worked.** (1) Feed the referent IMAGES to the Fable agent directly (it has Read) —
its plan re-anchored hard once it saw the owner's actual references (spread-display, streaming plume,
molten-on-black). (2) Surface the ONE load-bearing creative decision to the owner before banking — here,
"hero = the streaming train (rear-chase-native, inverts Solar) vs the front wing-spread of the refs."
The owner picked train-hero; everything else follows from it. Don't write a full frozen build sheet
before the load-bearing choice is blessed.

**What it unlocks.** Phoenix has a buildable contract that clears not-M / not-Solar / not-tacky-fire /
rear-chase / roster-distinct from line one — the whole point of the method. For Obsidian and Pearl,
reach for compositional inversion (mass to a different frame region) + a different profile-function
family as the first distinctiveness moves, before touching palette.
