# Vesper I3 — the cat head + split-fan tail: nubs not needles, eyes on the BROW

**What we did.** Built `vesperCatHead` (blunt knapped cat-wedge, big acid-green almond eyes,
ear-fin nubs, no horns) + `splitFanTail` (chined stem → twin split fan-fins, port-fin
white-speckle constellation, NO red prosthetic). Fable gate 4.3/5 (from a 3.7 FAIL). Verified:
smoke · wingsymprobe Δ0.000 · tricount monotonic <6000.

**Lesson 1 — a thin flat triangle standing on the occiput ALWAYS reads as a spike.** First
pass built ear-fins as tall thin blades → a spiky mohawk/fringe, worse with 3 pairs. A cat-ear
NUB is SHORT + BROAD (width ≈ height), swept, canted ±12° off-sagittal (base offset in z tilts
the face toward the rear cam so it reads from behind). The front pair is the dominant "ears",
later ladder pairs a smaller crest — never a row of equal needles. Same disease/cure as the
tail petals: bold + broad reads as a knapped fin; thin + tall reads as a quill.

**Lesson 2 — the headline feature must hold in the headline ANGLE.** The eyes are the head's
whole budget, but they were placed LOW + LATERAL on the muzzle → from the front they read as lit
nostrils and the whelp out-read the adult. The eyes are a face-FRONT claim, so they must sit
HIGH on the brow plane, be CONVERGED forward (rotation.y toward camera so both almonds face the
lens), and be genuinely large. Judge the eye on the face-front tile, not the flattering ¾.

**Lesson 3 — "acid-green" that renders as butter is a hue+red-channel problem.** 0x96d62a
(R150 G214 B42, hue ~82°) is a YELLOW-green; under an emissive eye + bloom it washes to
butter/sulfur. Drop the red channel and shift ~20° greener (→ ~0x6ae62a, hue ~100°) for a TRUE
saturated acid-green that survives the lighting. Re-check cruise-black after saturating: a
brighter diffuse/emissive eye risks blowing out on the dark tile — here it held (eyes stayed the
sole strong emitter), but that's the risk to verify.

**Lesson 4 — the TWIN read needs a visible center GAP, and it doubles as the anti-fray fix.**
Twin split fan-fins built too close together read as one symmetric spray, and thin petals read
as a frayed brush. Separating the port/starboard clusters (rootX ±0.13) makes the "twin" legible
AND, with wider knapped-diamond petals, kills the brush — one change, two fixes. The Night-Fury
"one fin is different" nod is an ASYMMETRY OF MARKING (port fin carries the speckle
constellation), NOT a red prosthetic — fresh, legally clean, palette-safe, zero extra silhouette.

**Process.** Same resumed high-effort Fable harsh-critic (3.7 FAIL → 4.3 PASS). It caught that
the head's headline feature failed in the head's headline angle even though ¾/profile looked
fine — a fresh spawn judging only the flattering angle would have missed it. Took one endorsed
polish (eye +10% for the "roster's largest eye" identity claim) after the gate opened.

**What it unlocks.** Head + tail now share the torso's knapped-facet + value-band language; the
whole creature reads as one cohesive premium night-drake. The dorsal ridge (torso), the fin rims
(tail), and the wing-root spark line are the anchors the I4 Starlit Seam lights (Surge-only).
