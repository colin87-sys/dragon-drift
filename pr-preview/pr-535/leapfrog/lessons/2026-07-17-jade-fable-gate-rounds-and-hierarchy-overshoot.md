# 2026-07-17 — Jade AAA: the harsh-Fable gate rounds + the streamer-hierarchy overshoot

**Did / learned.** Ran the buildsheet's per-increment harsh-critic gate on the finished Jade build
(builder never self-judges — spawned a FRESH high-effort Fable critic each round, judged on real
rear-chase silhouettes + the colour ladder + machine numbers, ≥4.2 to pass). Two rounds:

- **r0 → 3.6/5 FAIL** (no veto). The hero caudal fan read as a rounded fishtail/heart, not a
  countable split blossom; the fin-tip dew gems floated past the blade tips in the money frame; the
  streamers were 1px hairlines and the deep-emerald DARK tier was under-represented.
- **r1 → 4.0/5 FAIL** (near-miss, "craft not concept"). Fixes that LANDED: gems reseated onto the
  blade; fan-root deepened to restore the dark tier; split deep enough to break the fishtail (two
  prongs now countable). But the streamer widen **OVERSHOT** — the fattened flank ribbons now
  out-massed the hero caudal fan and inverted the silhouette hierarchy (the eye read the "bloom" at
  the flanks, not the tail). r2 eased the streamer band back (1.08→0.96).

The residual gap to 4.2 is **not headless-closable**: the caudal fan measures ~0.27× the pectoral
V in the NEUTRAL silhouette cam vs the §8.9 ≥0.55× target — but that target is explicitly
*perspective-assisted* (the tail rides nearest the chase lens; FIDELITY DRIFT 2), so only the real
in-game chase frame can prove it. Growing the fan further to force the ratio in a neutral cam IS the
OWNER-APPROVAL silhouette-growth lever (§3a.7) — not a self-sanctioned tweak. Plus the pearl reads as
a chrome ball in the static showcase (its breath tick was dead until this build's B1 fix and only
animates live). So the honest resolution is: land the headless-closable craft fixes, then route the
size/perspective/motion calls to the owner + the live preview.

**→ Systematize.** (1) **A harsh critic per increment genuinely raises the bar** — r0's 3.6 caught a
fishtail the builder's own eye had rationalised as "a split fan"; the builder must NOT grade its own
art (the soft-gate lesson, made real). Spawn it fresh each round so it doesn't anchor on the prior
verdict, and relay its ACTUAL output, never a self-flattering paraphrase. (2) **Every silhouette
lever has a mass budget — widening one element steals hero-presence from another.** The streamer
widen fixed the thin-thread tell but stole the caudal fan's hierarchy; fixing a cheap-tell can
manufacture a worse one (hero-inversion). Check the WHOLE-frame mass balance after any width change,
not just the element you touched. (3) **Know which gate misses are headless-closable vs
owner/preview calls BEFORE spinning more rounds** — a neutral-cam ratio that depends on perspective,
a pearl that only glows in motion, and an outline-growth that needs sign-off are all things more
silhouette iterations CANNOT resolve; recognising that stops the builder from grinding the mask and
sends the real decision to the human. (4) **Diagnose "holes" with `silhouette --holes`** — it
quantifies interior through-holes + fill-component count, turning a critic's "venetian-blind
rhombic grid" vibe into 8 holes / 4.7% planform / 3 fill components (the 3 separate fan blades +
streamers), so you can tell an intended gap from a winding defect.

**→ Leapfrog.** The build is headless-complete at a Fable 4.0 near-miss with every machine gate
green; the last 0.2 lives in the owner's silhouette-growth appetite and the live-preview motion the
stills structurally cannot show. The reusable asset for the next dragon: this exact gate cadence
(fresh critic per round + the headless-vs-preview triage) plus the mass-balance check as a standing
step after any silhouette-lever change.
