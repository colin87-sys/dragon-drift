# 2026-07-16 — graphics: PART 0, the standing AWE DOCTRINE in the biome playbook

**What we did.** Added a standing top-level part — **PART 0 — THE AWE DOCTRINE** — to
`reforged/BIOME-OVERHAUL-PLAYBOOK.md`, placed between the read-first stack and Part A
so every fresh overhaul session hits it before the pipeline. It codifies, as owner
doctrine for EVERY biome overhaul (not any one biome):

1. **The awe bar (0.1)** — the primary success criterion is a player thinking "wow,
   this game is absolutely beautiful" (AAA-premium). Explicit rank order: **4.2/5 is
   the FLOOR (pass gate); AWE is the TARGET.** The ossuary is cited as the proof-shape
   of "all gates green, bar missed."
2. **Scene composition as a first-class deliverable (0.2)** — a named composition
   script per biome (framing, depth layering, silhouette, COLOR SCRIPT, light/
   time-of-day mood, atmosphere, reflections/water, focal hierarchy, negative space,
   and the MOTION read at flight speed), with **its own Fable checkpoint** scored on
   moving in-context captures — element scores never substitute for it.
3. **The reference north star (0.3)** — Ghost of Tsushima × Breath of the Wild, shot
   like National Geographic — studied for PRINCIPLES (composition, color, light,
   mood), never content or assets.
4. **The research → Fable-synthesizes workflow (0.4)** — Fable is the active art
   director owning the vision end to end; Fable decides WHAT/HOW to research and
   **delegates the gathering to Opus agents** (reference-game composition + real-world
   /NatGeo references for the biome's subject); Opus reports; **Fable synthesizes**
   into the bible/color script/roster/build order; Fable stays director at every
   checkpoint.
5. **Awe never licenses replication (0.5)** — explicit two-way binding to Part B: no
   copying reference-game content, no replicating another Dragon Drift biome's look;
   beauty derives from THIS biome's identity, material, and light.

Cross-wired it so it can't be skipped: intro pointer before Part A; A1 Stage 1 now

**What we learned / the gotcha.** The playbook already had a strong QUALITY floor
(4.2/5 per element) but no stated TARGET above it — and the Frozen arc proved a floor
without a target ships mediocrity with green checkmarks (the ossuary passed every
gate). A floor and a target are different instruments: the floor filters elements, the
target judges the assembled scene. If the doc only names the floor, sessions optimize
to it. Second gotcha: composition had no owner — props, hazards, and atmosphere each
had gates, but the SCENE (what the player actually sees at speed) was nobody's
deliverable, so it got no score.

**The reusable pattern.** For any quality doctrine: state the floor and the target as
separate, explicitly ranked criteria; make the emergent whole (composition) a named
deliverable with its own checkpoint, not a hoped-for side effect; and pair every
"be more ambitious" doctrine with the constraint that bounds it (awe ↔
anti-replication), each citing the other, so neither can be quoted alone as a loophole.
Also the delegation shape: the director (Fable) specifies and synthesizes research;
the gatherer (Opus) fans out and reports — directors direct, they don't grep the web.

**What it unlocks.** Every future biome overhaul (Caldera next) starts with an
explicit AAA beauty bar, a research-driven art-direction workflow, and a composition
gate — instead of rediscovering per biome that "all elements passed" ≠ "beautiful."
The AWE-SOURCE checklist row gives Gate-2 reviewers a mechanical way to reject
borrowed beauty.
